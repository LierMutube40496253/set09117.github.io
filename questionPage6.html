You are the middle player and the setter has told you to do the next hit at first tempo , what does that mean?

The first tempo is the faster hit, which means you will have to jump before the setter touches the ball and be at your max height just when the setter is touching it.

Start the "batida" once the setter has touched the ball

Jump just after the setter touches the ball

Jump before the setter touches the ball and be in the air when the setter is touching the ball

Move two meters behind the setter and wait until the set is on the proper high to start the "batida".
